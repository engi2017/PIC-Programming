Tutorial 7 - Blinking LED with Time Delay

  1. Start off with placing the PIC16F684 and the dip where your PICkit 3 will be connected an appropriate distance apart on the breadboard.
  2. To make the breadboard neater during circuit construction, make power rails. Connect a wire to and from the positive and negative sides of the breadboard. This will allow you to have a voltage of 5V on both sides of the breadboard.
    3. Note: The power rails are connected internally. The columns within the main area of the board are interconnected, so you can insert a wire into one hole and it will be electrically connected to a neighboring hole (vertical connections for the numbered columns, and horizontal connections for the power rails).
  3. Connect a voltage regulator to decrease the voltage as the circuit requires 5V of the 9V in the battery.
    4. a. The input pin will be attached to the positive side of the 9V battery. b. The ground pin will go to ground. c. The output pin will go to Vcc.
  4. Connect pin 2 on the PICkit to Vcc and pin 3 on the PICkit to ground.
  5. Connect pin 1 on the chip to Vcc and pin 14 on the chip to ground.
  6. Connect pin 4 on the chip to pin 1 on the PICkit (Vpp).
  7. Connect pin 13 on the chip to pin 4 on the PICkit (ICSPDAT).
  8. Connect pin 12 on the chip to pin 5 on the PICkit (ICSPCLK).
  9. Connect a 220 W resistor to Pin 10 on the chip which is then connected to the positive leg of the LED. The negative leg of the LED is connected to ground.
  10. Attach the battery to the breadboard connecting the positive end to the input pin on the voltage regulator and the negative end to any negative area on the breadboard.
